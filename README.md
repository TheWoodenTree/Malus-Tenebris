# Malus Tenebris

First-person survival horror set in an underground medieval cult prison. Built in Godot.

## What's implemented

- Basic enemy AI (wander/chase/attack state machine)
- Vitriscet time mechanic causes your mind to deteriorate (tracked via an hourglass)
- Safe room with distillation mechanics using collected resources to prevent Vitriscet from worsening
- Tutorial area and central prison hub
- Player movement and interaction systems

## In progress

- Enemy territorial behavior and threat escalation
- Level design for prison sections
- Non-euclidean spatial design for deeper areas

## About the game

1209 AD

How many innocent people will it take, you wonder. You cannot sit idly by while your fellow countrymen are rounded up like animals, accused of crimes too heinous to believe by the vile bastard who calls himself praetor.

You would like nothing more than to see his neck under the cold steel of an axe.

You spoke out. Now, you find yourself among the unfortunate souls taken to the old prison, nestled deep within the wilderness of the heart of the island, sitting, waiting for your fate inside a cold, wet cell.

But your suspicions only grow stronger.

An indescribable malevolence permeates the stale prison air. You are overwhelmed with a sense of unease so strong it's almost painful.

When the incessant whispers of the nearby guards one day vanishes, you find yourself completely alone, with the deafening silence held at bay only by the crackling flames of hanging lamp in your cell.

Finding a small passage, you escape your cell and find the exit wide open. You can see sunlight pouring in from the outside. But you can't leave just yet.

Fueled by contempt for your captors, you feel an insatiable desire to descend deeper into the strangely enticing darkness.

You must uncover the truth.

---

You become trapped in an underground prison after escaping your cell and deciding to go deeper in search of the truth. The deeper you explore, the stranger things become. Manage your deteriorating mind while searching for a way out and avoiding the animated ashen remains of the cultists left inside the prison.
